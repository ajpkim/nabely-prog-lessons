History of Paper

Paper was invented in ancient China during the early 2nd century AD. Originally made from mulberry and other bast fibres along with fishnets, old rags, and hemp waste.

Key Developments

  • 105 AD: Papermaking invented by Cai Lun in China.
  • 3rd century: Spread of papermaking to Korea and Japan.
  • 12th century: Papermaking spread to Europe via the Islamic world.
  • 19th century: Introduction of wood-based papers.

Modern Paper Usage

Today, paper is ubiquitous, used for printing, writing, packaging, and numerous other purposes. The technology and materials continue to evolve, emphasizing sustainability and recycling.
